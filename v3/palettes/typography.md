# Palette: Typography
Select ONE per build. Log the ID in PALETTE_BLOCK.
Sets font pairing, scale philosophy, and type personality.
Never use Arial, Helvetica, Times New Roman, or Georgia.

---

## T1 — Serif Drama / Sans Utility
Display font: high-contrast serif (Playfair Display, Cormorant Garamond, or Instrument Serif)
Body font: clean geometric sans (Inter, DM Sans, or Space Grotesk)
Scale: extreme — display at 96px+, body at 16px. Maximum ratio.
Tracking: tight on headlines (-0.03em), wide on labels (+0.12em)
**Use when:** premium, luxury, editorial, barber shops, restaurants, hospitality, studios.
**Avoid when:** tech products, data tools, youth/energetic brands.
**Headline feel:** Commanding. Each word carries weight.
**Google Fonts:** `Playfair Display` + `DM Sans` OR `Cormorant Garamond` + `Inter`

---

## T2 — Condensed Sans / Bold Impact
Display font: condensed or ultra-wide sans (Bebas Neue, Barlow Condensed, or Black Han Sans)
Body font: neutral readable sans (Inter, Roboto, or Source Sans 3)
Scale: extreme width contrast — very tall, narrow headline vs normal body.
Tracking: display at 0 or +0.04em (condenser benefits from slight spread), body normal.
**Use when:** sports, streetwear, food brands, barber shops, urban culture, direct-response.
**Avoid when:** luxury-soft, elegant, corporate formal.
**Headline feel:** Loud, confident, takes up space deliberately.
**Google Fonts:** `Bebas Neue` + `Inter` OR `Barlow Condensed:700` + `Source Sans 3`

---

## T3 — Mono / Technical
Display font: monospace (Space Mono, JetBrains Mono, or IBM Plex Mono)
Body font: humanist sans (IBM Plex Sans, Space Grotesk)
Scale: moderate — mono doesn't scale to extremes well. Max 64px display.
Tracking: slightly wide on mono (+0.05em). Tight on body.
**Use when:** tech, developer tools, data products, industrial, architectural, editorial-cold.
**Avoid when:** warm brands, service businesses, premium luxury.
**Headline feel:** Precise, technical, communicates competence over emotion.
**Google Fonts:** `Space Mono` + `Space Grotesk` OR `JetBrains Mono` + `IBM Plex Sans`

---

## T4 — Variable / Expressive
Display font: variable font with personality (Fraunces, Raleway:700, or Syne)
Body font: humanist sans (Nunito Sans, Outfit, or Plus Jakarta Sans)
Scale: moderate-extreme. Variable axes allow width-stretch or weight-pull for effect.
Tracking: depends on use — wide for labels, tight for display.
**Use when:** creative agencies, portfolio, lifestyle brands, wellness, boutique retail.
**Avoid when:** strictly formal, utility-first, corporate trust-critical.
**Headline feel:** Has personality — the font itself communicates brand character.
**Google Fonts:** `Fraunces` + `Nunito Sans` OR `Syne:700` + `Plus Jakarta Sans`

---

## T5 — All-Caps / Brutal
Display font: heavy all-caps with no apology (Anton, Black Ops One, or Oswald:700)
Body font: clean legible sans (Lato, Open Sans)
Scale: moderate-extreme. All-caps already reads as large — don't push too far.
Tracking: all-caps ALWAYS get +0.08em or wider to breathe properly.
**Use when:** streetwear, nightlife, underground brands, bold food brands, activism.
**Avoid when:** professional services, any brand that needs to feel approachable or soft.
**Headline feel:** No negotiation. Direct. Occupies space unapologetically.
**Google Fonts:** `Anton` + `Lato` OR `Oswald:700` + `Open Sans`
